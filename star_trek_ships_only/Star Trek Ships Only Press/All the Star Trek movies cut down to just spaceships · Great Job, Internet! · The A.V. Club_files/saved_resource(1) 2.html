                                                                                                                                            I actually think, in abstract terms, TNG did space battles best. Unlike the movies or DS9, the ships were always assumed to be very, very far apart for one another (any closeness on screen was for the audience’s benefit) and a lot of it would be conveyed via Worf informing whether they hit or not. And, since the distances were so huge, your options were either hit or miss, with the amount of damage being determined by how well shields held up. And there also seemed to be an assumption that, if shields dropped, you were screwed—a lot of TNG confrontations (I’m thinking in particular whenever the Enterprise has to stare down a warbird) get most of the feeling of mutually assured destruction, and that actually opening fire would mean entering a state of war.

                                                                                                                                            A lot pf that was because of budget limitations, but (as much as I love DS9 and think starship battles are fun to watch) I think Trek lost a something unique when it went back towards the more skirmish-y stuff as effects got better.
