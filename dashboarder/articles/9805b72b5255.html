Kabir Bedi celebrates 50 years of Sandokan at Sanremo Music Festival: "All thanks to the great late Sergio Sollima"

One of the most defining moments of his career came with the iconic 1976 Italian television series Sandokan, directed by Sergio Sollima. The series not only became hugely popular in Europe but also made history, as Bedi became one of the first Indian actors to headline a major international television show. His portrayal of the legendary pirate hero turned him into a global sensation and left a lasting impact on audiences worldwide.

Recently, the beloved series marked its 50th anniversary, and the milestone was celebrated at the prestigious Sanremo Music Festival in Italy. Kabir Bedi attended the celebration and shared glimpses of the special moment on social media. He posted photos from the event with Turkish actor Can Yaman, who is set to play the iconic role in the upcoming adaptation of Sandokan. Bedi also expressed his gratitude to the creators and director who brought the series to life, calling the anniversary celebration a deeply moving and memorable moment in his journey.

Sharing pictures, he wrote, “Celebrated the 50th anniversary of my Sandokan series at the San Remo Festival in Italy on February 24th, and coming together with the dynamic new Sandokan Can Yaman, all broadcast live on RAI. LINK TO EVENT IN BIO. A moving moment for me! I never imagined I’d be in San Remo 50 years later, all thanks to the great late Sergio Sollima, who created and directed the series from the eternal fantastic stories of Emilio Salgari. Thank you San Remo, RAI, Carlo Conti, Laura Fausini and Can Yaman 🇮🇹 #blessed #Sandokan #sanremo #italia #actorslife”

Sandokan was based on the famous adventure novels written by Emilio Salgari, which revolve around the legendary pirate hero Sandokan. The series went on to become a massive international success and turned Kabir Bedi into a global icon, and his powerful portrayal of the fearless pirate won millions of admirers.

Even Bollywood superstar Salman Khan recently celebrated this remarkable milestone achieved by his close friend Kabir Bedi. Marking nearly five decades of the iconic show, Salman congratulated the veteran actor for creating history with the series and bringing global recognition to Indian talent. In a warm and heartfelt gesture, Salman praised Bedi for his unforgettable performance and referred to him as the “original Tiger of India.”
