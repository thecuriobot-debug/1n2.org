Pakistan’s shaky start with the bat

Pakistan opted to bat first but quickly found themselves under pressure. Wickets kept falling at regular intervals and the scoreboard barely moved during the early overs.At one stage, Pakistan had lost nine wickets for only 82 runs, raising the possibility of being dismissed for under 100. Faheem Ashraf then provided some resistance and pushed the total past the 100-run mark with a late effort.

Several experienced names were missing from the playing XI as the team management made significant changes for the series.

Big changes in Pakistan squad

For the Bangladesh tour, Pakistan dropped senior players including Babar Azam, Fakhar Zaman and Saim Ayub. The selectors decided to test new faces as the team begins preparations for the 2027 ODI World Cup.Debutants fail to convert starts

Despite the hype around the new players, the debutants could not make a big impact in the opening match.Sahibzada Farhan, who was the top run-scorer in the T20 World Cup, managed 27 runs. Maaz Sadaqat scored 18 on debut, while wicketkeeper Mohammad Rizwan also struggled and scored just 10 runs.

The early dismissals left Pakistan in trouble and exposed the fragile batting order once again.

Shaheen Afridi explains decision to give debuts

Before the match, Shaheen Afridi had spoken about the decision to hand opportunities to new players and expressed confidence in their ability to perform.“There will be like three debuts, of course. Sahibzada is the top performer in T20s and also in List ‘A’ cricket for Pakistan. Maaz Sadaqat will open, and Shamyl Hussain will play at one down. These three will make their debut. Hopefully, they will play their own game and show their skills,” Afridi said in the pre-match press conference as quoted by The Daily Star.

He added that the team management is looking beyond just this series.

“We are excited to see them play for Pakistan, not just for one series but with the future in mind. They have experience playing here. They have played the Bangladesh league here. Sahibzada and Maaz have played here, and a few others were also involved last year. They are familiar with the grounds and conditions, and I hope they will play their best cricket,” he added.

Focus shifts to ODI World Cup preparation

Afridi also said Pakistan expects a challenging contest against Bangladesh as both teams look ahead to the 2027 ODI World Cup scheduled in South Africa.“Any team is strong at home. Bangladesh are a good side, and they played well against the West Indies. But we are also ready. We performed well in the last two ODI series against South Africa and Sri Lanka, and we want to continue that,” he said.
