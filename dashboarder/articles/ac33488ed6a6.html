Air New Zealand to cut flights amid rising fuel prices

Air New Zealand said on Thursday it would cancel about 5% of its flights, roughly about 1,100, through early May as fallout from the Iran war drove up jet fuel prices and restricted flight routes.

The airline said around 44,000 passengers out of 1.9 million scheduled to travel during the period would need to be rebooked, with most cuts affecting domestic routes.

Chief executive Nikhil Ravishankar told Radio New Zealand that long-haul flights would largely be preserved.

"People want to get to Europe still, and over the US airspace we can get them into Europe, and that's what we're focused on doing," he said.

Air New Zealand and other carriers, including Australia's Qantas Airways, Scandinavia's SAS and Thai Airways, have also raised fares this week as the conflict disrupted Middle East airspace and pushed up fuel costs.

Meanwhile, authorities in New Zealand said on Thursday they are mulling restricting vehicle use if fuel supplies drop due to the Iran war.

Oman evacuates vessels from key oil expert terminal, Mina al Fahal — Bloomberg

Oman has evacuated all vessels from its key oil export terminal at Mina Al Fahal as a precautionary measure, Bloomberg News reported on Thursday.

Bloomberg cited direct recipients of a port agent's notice.

Mina Al Fahal is strategically located in the Gulf of Oman, outside of the Strait of Hormuz, which is currently blocked by Iran.

That makes it one of the few remaining ports providing an alternative route to ship crude oil from the Middle East.

The evacuation orders came after drone strikes triggered a fire at the Port of Salalah, Oman's biggest port, on Wednesday.

The state-run Oman News Agency reported around midnight local time (2000 GMT/UST) that teams were responding to a fire at the port after earlier saying "multiple unmanned aerial vehicles" were intercepted at Salalah Port.

"Efforts to contain the fire … may take some time," it said.

Iraq halts operations at all the country's oil terminals

Iraq said on Thursday that it had temporarily stopped operations at all of its oil terminals.

The closures came after two foreign oil tankers were attacked and caught fire in Iraqi territorial waters near the country's southern export terminals.

The two vessels were operating near Iraq's Al-Faw port area in the north of the Persian Gulf when the attacks occurred.

Farhan Al-Fartousi from Iraq's General Company for Ports told state television that one crew member had been killed and 38 rescued from the ships.

Iraqi firefighters have since extinguished the fires on both ships.

The ships are the Maltese-flagged Zefyros and the Safesea Vishnu, flying the Marshall Islands flag.

Al-Fartousi made no mention of the dead crew member's nationality or ship.

Rescue teams continue to search for missing seafarers, he said.

The Iraqi Oil Marketing Company (SOMO) warned in a statement that the incident "negatively affects Iraq’s security and economy, and poses a threat to the safety of maritime navigation and oil activities in Iraqi regional waters."

Oil prices surge again with Brent crude oil hitting $100 a barrel

Brent crude oil futures, the international standard, were trading about 9% higher on Thursday at $100.51.

Brent crude oil futures had fallen in the past few days.

But it surged again amid concerns following Iranian attacks on commercial shipping and the closure of oil terminals around the Gulf.

This is despite the International Energy Agency agreeing on Wednesday to release 400 million barrels of oil.

Iran has targeted oil fields and refineries in Gulf Arab nations and effectively stopped cargo traffic through the Strait of Hormuz.

A fifth of all traded oil passes through the narrow sea passage.
