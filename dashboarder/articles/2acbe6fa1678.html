Manchester City face Real Madrid in the Champions League round of 16, the sixth time the clubs have met in a knockout tie in the competition in the past seven seasons.

City and Madrid's all-time head-to-head is dead even, with five wins for each and five draws across 15 meetings dating back to a dramatic win for Los Blancos at the Santiago Bernabeu in 2012.

In terms of their five two-legged ties spanning 2020 until last season's knockout playoff clash, Madrid have progressed on three occasions. The teams also met in the league phase this season, when Nico O'Reilly cancelled out Rodrygo's opener before Erling Haaland's first-half penalty sealed a 2-1 win.

The first leg of this game in the Spanish capital comes with City and Madrid each currently second best behind Arsenal and Barcelona in their domestic title races.

Below, The Sporting News looks at Man City's latest injury picture.

Man City injury list

Below is the latest injury picture for Manchester City, and estimated return dates for those who are sidelined.

Erling Haaland

- Injury: Shin

- Expected return date: March 11 (vs. Real Madrid)

Haaland sat out the 3-1 FA Cup fifth-round win over the weekend and then caused consternation among City fans by posting a picture of his bloodied shin on social media, apparently caused by Mateo Kovacic during training. But, speaking after the victory at St James' Park, Guardiola said Haaland had simply been left out to manage his workload in light of his recent ankle problem.

Rico Lewis

- Injury: Ankle

- Expected return date: March 11 (vs. Real Madrid)

Lewis would have expected to enjoy some action as a rotation option at Newcastle, but the versatile backup was absent with a swollen ankle.

Josko Gvardiol

- Injury: Tibia fracture

- Expected return date: May/June

Man City suffered a brutal blow in the 1-1 draw with Chelsea, as the opening match of 2026 brought with it terrible news. Defender Gvardiol, who has been a pillar of consistency for City in recent seasons, went down in the second half with a tibia fracture, confirmed by the club the following day.

City confirmed that Gvardiol underwent surgery for the broken leg. While there is very little known at this point about a potential timeline for Gvardiol's return, it is believed to require a long-term recovery, and it's possible he will miss the remainder of the 2025/26 season.

Mateo Kovacic

- Injury: Heel/ankle

- Expected return date: March

Croatia midfielder Kovacic underwent Achilles surgery over the summer but has suffered a serious setback.

After the 3-1 win over Bournemouth on November 2, Guardiola said the former Chelsea player would be "out for a while".

"He still has a problem with the ankle, a little calcification still there. We'll wait on him for the last part of the season," the City boss added. Croatian newspaper Sportske Novosti confirmed the news and said Kovacic's recovery was likely to take three to four months. In early March, the former Chelsea and Real Madrid man returned to first-team training.
