![LPG crisis india](https://img.etimg.com/thumb/msid-129577559,width-300,height-225,imgsize-285328,resizemode-75/lpg-crisis-india.jpg)

The demand comes after M. K. Stalin, Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu, announced a relief of ₹2 per unit of electricity for every additional unit consumed by the hotel industry to help it cope with the shortage of commercial LPG cylinders. Supplies have gone haywire after war broke out in the Persian Gulf.

In Tamil Nadu, the subsidy applies to hotels, tea stalls and other eateries that switch to electric stoves instead of LPG cylinders, and will remain in force until the Union government eases supply restrictions on commercial LPG.

The Bengaluru body has sought a similar relief package from the Karnataka government until LPG supplies return to normal.

Hotels and eateries across Bengaluru have adopted several measures to cope with the shortage, including pruning menus, avoiding deep-fried items, shortening operating hours or opening only in the evenings. Several establishments that have exhausted their LPG stocks have temporarily shut operations, putting up notices for customers.

“The State Government distributes them once supplied by the Centre. The Food Minister has already held two meetings on the issue,” he said.

The Chief Minister added that the State has written to the Union Petroleum Minister urging immediate steps to prevent any shortage of LPG cylinders.
