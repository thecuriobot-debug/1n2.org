Nigeria's financial markets enter the week of 9 March 2026 on a cautiously constructive footing, but the signals beneath the surface demand careful disaggregation. The Stanbic IBTC PMI's rebound to 53.2 confirms that private sector momentum is real; Q4 2025 GDP data reinforces a recovery cycle that is broadening. Yet the more critical discipline for the week ahead lies in connecting growth confirmation to the pace of fiscal and credit intermediation, a reconnection that policy has not yet fully executed.

Capital market breadth remains selective, with index gains concentrated in energy counters rather than distributed across sectors, a divergence that informed positioning cannot overlook. Dangote Cement's deleveraging story underscores how balance-sheet discipline, not revenue expansion alone, is unlocking earnings capacity in a high-cost environment.

Banking recapitalisation is advancing, but compliance progress should not be mistaken for systemic resilience until funding quality and credit transmission are also assessed.

Externally, the Iran conflict and its spillover into oil supply chains and global chip markets introduce tail risks that are not yet fully priced into domestic assumptions. Execution discipline, not optimism, should govern today's decisions.
