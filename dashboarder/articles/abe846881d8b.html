Middle East war live: Projectile strikes US embassy in Baghdad

![Smoke rises from the US embassy building in Baghdad](https://s.france24.com/media/display/794a3f1e-1f8b-11f1-9433-005056a90284/w:1024/p:16x9/US-embassy-Baghdad.jpg)

The US embassy in Baghdad was struck by a projectile on Saturday, with plumes of black smoke seen rising above the diplomatic mission. The attack came as the Iranian authorities vowed to retaliate to US attacks on Kharg Island that President Donald Trump said had “obliterated” military infrastructure. Follow our liveblog for the latest updates.

- A blast rocked a major pro-Palestinian rally in Tehran on Friday after Israel warned it could target the area.

- a US official said that roughly 2,500 Marines and at least one amphibious assault ship were headed for the Middle East in a major addition of troops.

- US Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth said that Iran's new supreme leader Mojtaba Khamenei was wounded and "likely disfigured", casting doubt on his ability to lead after nearly two weeks of conflict.

- The humanitarian crisis in Lebanon deepened, with nearly 800 people killed and 850,000 displaced as Israel launched fresh waves of strikes against Iran-backed Hezbollah militants.

- A drone strike killed a French soldier in Iraq's autonomous Kurdistan region, France's armed forces ministry said, marking the country's first military death in the Middle East war.
