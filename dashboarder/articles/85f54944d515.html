During his final season with the Chicago Bulls, Michael Jordan won his sixth NBA Championship after defeating the Utah Jazz. At the height of his career in Chicago, Jordan retired from the NBA for the second time in his career.

For his “last dance” with the Bulls, MJ was wearing a pair of Nike Air Jordan 13 signature shoes. And now, Nike is rereleasing the sneakers to celebrate Jordan’s last season in Chicago.

The sports apparel company is set to reintroduce the Air Jordan 13 sneakers, originally released for the 1997-98 NBA season, in a new “Chicago” colorway. The sneakers drop on Saturday, Mar. 14 at 10 a.m. ET/7 a.m. PT.

Priced at $215 per pair, the Nike Air Jordan 13 sneakers are designed from premium materials, such as authentic leather, as well as a lush and rich textile-material upper with a fresh look and clean feel that has an old school look with a modern appeal.

In addition, the sneakers have the iconic “Jumpman” logo on their tongues, the “Jordan” branding at the base of the laces, “Zoom Air” bubble in each midsole and prominent quilted overlay on the quarter panels. They even have a green holographic eye with Jordan’s logo at the outer collars. These sneakers have a classic look with loads of style that works just as well nearly 30 years later in 2026, as they did back in 1997.

The Nike Air Jordan 13 Retro “Chicago” sneakers come in a white, true red and black colorway (Chicago Bulls colors) that’s ideal for on-the-court, or casual use.
