Prime Minister Narendra Modi will release the 22nd instalment of the PM Kisan scheme today ( March 13, 2026, Friday). Under this installment, an amount of more than Rs 18,640 crore will be transferred directly into the bank accounts of more than 9.32 crore farmer families across the country through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT).

Modi released the 21st instalment of the PM Kisan scheme on November 19, 2025, from Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu.

Union Minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan stated that since the scheme's launch in February 2019 under the leadership of Prime Minister Shri Narendra Modi, more than Rs 4.09 lakh crore has been directly deposited to the accounts of qualified farmer households. With the delivery of the 22nd instalment, the cumulative amount transferred will reach Rs 4.27 lakh crore, says Chouhan

Under this scheme, eligible farmers are provided with a financial assistance of Rs 6,000 per year in three equal instalments of Rs 2,000, which helps them meet their needs for seeds, fertilisers, equipment and other agricultural requirements.

The government has identified certain suspected cases that may be excluded from the PM Kisan scheme beneficiary list. These include:

* Farmers who acquired land ownership after February 1, 2019.

* Cases where more than one family member is receiving benefits (e.g., both husband and wife, an adult member, minor, etc.).

* The benefits for such cases have been temporarily withheld till their physical verification is completed.

In the 22nd installment alone, over 2.15 crore women farmer beneficiaries will receive financial assistance.

Union Minister informed that to simplify the e-KYC process, a face-authentication-based mobile app has been launched. Through this app, farmers can complete their e-KYC from home without the need for an OTP or biometric device.

Is eKYC mandatory?

eKYC is mandatory for all PM Kisan-registered farmers. OTP-based eKYC is provided on the PM Kisan portal and nearest CSC centers can be contacted for biometric eKYC, as per the PM Kisan website.
