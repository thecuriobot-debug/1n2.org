Indiaâs proposed Draft Income Tax Rules 2026 might offer significant relief for salaried taxpayers sticking with the old tax regime, pending parliamentary approval. The draft tax rules aim to broaden various tax exemptions and allowances, especially concerning certain components and perks for salaried individuals, which can lower the taxable income and thus reduce your net income tax payable.

However, most of the income tax changes proposed under the draft tax rules, 2026, are applicable for the old tax regime. Experts point out that these changes could enhance the competitiveness of the old tax regime once more, especially for those taxpayers who claim multiple tax deductions and allowances. While the majority of the proposed changes under the draft tax rules, 2026 reduce your total tax outgo, some may end up increasing the tax payable.

How the draft rules could increase salary exemptions

These are the proposed changes under the new draft income tax rules, 2026:

- House Rent Allowance (HRA)

- Children Education Allowance

- Hostel Allowance

- Employee perquisites like meals and gift vouchers

- Motor car perquisite (company vehicle)

- Interest-free or concessional loans to employees

- Transport Allowance for certain employees in Transport Systems

HRA exemption: What could change

Under House Rent Allowance (HRA) provisions, currently available only under the old tax regime, additional cities have been proposed to be classified as metro cities. If the draft tax rules are approved by Parliament without any modifications, taxpayers living in cities such as Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune, and Ahmedabad will become eligible to claim a higher HRA exemption.
