The ongoing crisis in West Asia serves as a stark reminder that the world we live in is far more fragile than we often care to admit. It's in times of global volatility that the true value of a mature democracy is revealed.

It's easy to criticise from the sidelines. But one must occasionally step back from the noise of annual balance sheets or 24-hr news cycles to look at progress through a decadal lens. India's journey over the last 10 yrs has not just been a story of growth, but a masterclass in democratic restraint and institutional maturity.

Democratic maturity is often invisible. It doesn't announce itself through dramatic headlines, but through steady, disciplined choices made over time. During the pandemic, many advanced economies treated their balance sheets like Monopoly money, unleashing unprecedented fiscal expansions. India's response, by contrast, reflected a remarkable degree of restraint.

Adoption of a formal inflation-targeting framework anchored expectations and brought stability to the macro environment. Over the past decade, inflation has averaged close to 5%, a dramatic improvement from the double-digit volatility that defined 2009-13. This shift has quietly transformed India's economic reputation from 'fragile economy' to being increasingly viewed as a 'fortress of stability'.

Equally significant has been GoI's strategic pivot toward capex - rising from 1.7% of GDP in 2016 to nearly 3.3% in 2025. This is a structural shift that prioritises long-term productivity over short-term political gratification.

Infrastructure is the quiet backbone of sovereignty. A resilient port network keeps trade moving when oceans grow uncertain. A strong power grid ensures factories continue to operate even when fuel markets fluctuate. Logistics corridors compress time across a continent-sized nation, unlocking productivity across thousands of miles.

Over the past decade, India has treated infra not merely as construction but as strategic capacity. New ports, expanded airports, RE parks, highways, rail corridors and digital networks have created a physical and tech foundation capable of supporting the ambitions of a $10 tn economy. Transformation is visible in ways statistics alone cannot capture. Cargo that once took days now moves in hours. Regions once disconnected now participate in national and global supply chains.

In much of the Western world, tech companies are often seen as rivals to the state. In India, the state chose a different path - it built tech as public infra. Result is the remarkable digital ecosystem, 'India Stack', a system that has quietly transformed how democracy interacts with its citizens.

Through Aadhaar, over a billion Indians now possess a verifiable digital identity. Through UPI, billions of financial transactions move seamlessly every month across the country. But the most profound transformation has occurred through the direct benefit transfer system. By linking Aadhaar with bank accounts and mobile networks, India eliminated layers of intermediaries that once absorbed a large share of welfare spending. Since its inception, more than ₹34 lakh cr has been transferred directly to citizens.

India's electorate is becoming increasingly performance-driven. Voters are rewarding outcomes - electricity in homes, roads connecting villages, reliable water supply - rather than relying solely on identity or ideology.

Perhaps the clearest sign of India's growing maturity lies in its foreign policy. The past decade has been marked by sharp geopolitical polarisation of 'us' and 'them'. India has resisted that binary. Whether navigating energy markets during ongoing conflicts, or championing the voice of developing economies in global fora, India has consistently pursued 'strategic autonomy' grounded in national interest.

Through fiscal discipline, strategic infrastructure investment, digital public infrastructure, and an unwavering commitment to democratic processes, India has demonstrated that stability and progress can advance together. Of course, challenges - from urbanisation pressures to employment generation - remain. But institutional foundations laid provide a far stronger platform to address them.

As the fires in West Asia remind us of the high cost of instability, India's calm, calculated ascent offers a different path, one where democratic values and decisive growth are not in conflict, but are the very engines of a nation's permanence. In a world searching for anchors, India is quietly becoming one.
