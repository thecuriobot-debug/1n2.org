Here is a roundup of the main developments in the US-Israeli war with Iran on Friday, March 13.

Dozens injured in missile attack on Israel

A missile attack injured 58 people in the regional council of Zarzir, located in Israel's far north, rescue services said.

One woman suffered moderate injuries, while 57 others were lightly injured from glass shards.

Zarzir is located near Israel's border with Lebanon.

Iranian-backed armed group Hezbollah said early Friday that it had fired several rocket salvos toward northern Israel and Israeli troops in southern Lebanon.

What to know if you're joining us now

Missile strikes have continued across the Middle East overnight and into this morning.

Saudi Arabia shot down drones early this morning. Sirens went off in Bahrain to warn residents to seek shelter.

Inside Iran, residents said security forces have increased their presence to demonstrate continued control, Reuters reported.

President Donald Trump issued a new threat to Iran, writing: "Watch what happens to these deranged scumbags today." He tallied the damaged inflicted on Iran and its leaders and called it a "great honor" to be responsible for it.

French President Emmanuel Macron said Thursday one soldier had died and several were wounded during an attack in northern Iraq, hours after an Italian base was also targeted in the area.

Trump says US 'totally destroying' Iran

President Donald Trump said the United States was "totally destroying" Iran's leadership and military capabilities, warning of a fresh wave of attacks.

"Watch what happens to these deranged scumbags today," Trump posted on his Truth Social platform, asserting that Tehran's navy and air force had been effectively wiped out, while the country is economically reeling.

In his late-night post, Trump said Iranian missiles and drones were being "decimated," and signaled further action against Tehran, saying the US has "unparalleled firepower, unlimited ammunition, and plenty of time."

"They've been killing innocent people all over the world for 47 years, and now I, as the 47th President of the United States of America, am killing them," Trump said. "What a great honor it is to do so!"

Injured US troops evacuated to Germany

About 19 injured US service members were flown to Germany on a military evacuation flight on Thursday, CBS News reported, quoting officials familiar with the matter.

The aircraft travelled to a US base near the Landstuhl Regional Medical Center, a major military hospital, from Saudi Arabia via Oman.

Among those evacuated were two troops wounded when a drone exploded near their vehicle.

Earlier, dozens of injured US service members were evacuated after a strike on a US facility in Kuwait. The attack had killed six people.

On Tuesday, about 20 troops arrived at the Landstuhl Regional Medical Center with "urgent" injuries, which include traumatic brain injuries, memory loss and concussions, the CBS News report said.

The hospital did not issue a comment on the topic.

Iraqi group says French interests to be targeted

A pro-Iranian group in Iraq warned on Friday that French interests "in Iraq and the region" would be targeted following the arrival of a French aircraft carrier.

The threat, posted on the Telegram channel of the Ashab Alkahf group, came after French President Emmanuel Macron announced that a French soldier had been killed and several others wounded in Iraq's Kurdistan region.

Earlier on Friday, France's president Emmanuel Macron called any attack on French forces unacceptable after a French soldier was killed in the Erbil region of Iraq. Several soldiers were also wounded in a drone attack.

"Their presence in Iraq is framed strictly within the fight against terrorism. The war in Iran cannot possibly justify such attacks," Macron wrote.

War nears two weeks as leaders vow to fight on

As the Iran war approached its two-week mark on Friday, the leaders of Iran, Israel and the United States struck defiant tones, even as the conflict claimed thousands of lives, rattled financial markets and sent oil prices soaring.

In his first public comments, read on state television, new Iranian Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei vowed to keep the Strait of Hormuz closed and warned neighboring countries to shut US bases or risk being targeted.

"I assure everyone that we will not neglect avenging the blood of your martyrs," he said.

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu defended US and Israeli airstrikes on Iran, saying the campaign is meant to help Iranians topple their leadership.

"We are creating the optimal conditions for toppling the regime but I won't deny that I can't tell you with all certainty that the people of Iran will topple the regime — a regime is toppled from the inside," Netanyahu said. "But we can definitely help and we are helping."

Meanwhile, US President Donald Trump said the United States and Israel had already "won" the war and argued that higher oil prices would benefit the US as the "largest Oil Producer in the World," while stressing the need to prevent Iran from obtaining nuclear weapons.

Trump's comment came as oil prices hovered around $100 a barrel, while US stocks saw major upheaval.
