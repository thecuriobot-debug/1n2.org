Chappell Roan turned into the paparazzi during Paris Fashion Week.

After captivating attention in a revealing, sheer piece at the McQueen Autumn Winter 2026 show on Sunday, March 8, the Grammy winner, 28, attempted to spend the rest of her night in an intimate fashion. She headed out for a night in the City of Lights dressed in an all-black outfit and a headscarf, seemingly trying to keep a low profile.

Despite her attempt, the singer exited her car at an unknown restaurant greeted by a swarm of fans and paparazzi taking her photo and shouting in her direction. Chappell reportedly asked the group several times to give her some space, but to no avail, causing her to turn the cameras in the opposite direction.

Chappell then began filming herself amid the group, saying, “This is what it’s like to be disregarded as a human. This is what it’s like.

“I’ve asked these people several times to get away from me,” she added, before turning to a fan asking for a photograph, pointing out, “This person, I’ve asked several times to go away and they will not. They’re hiding their face because they’re ashamed.”

She then proceeded to tell another to “go away” as she displayed all of the individuals “completely disregarding all of my boundaries."

After asking the group once again to stop “harassing” her, Chappell concluded, “So this is what it’s like if you’re wondering how it is. Thank you,” before storming into the restaurant.

Once the video went viral on X, many slammed her “attention seeking” behavior.

One said, “ok so leave the music industry. you knew what you were signing up for," while another added, “genz in a nutshell shes even being so rude to that one fan. just walk to your dinner wtf is wrong with you the paparazzi are working this is an ecosystem that YOU as a celebrity are a part of.”

A third questioned, “Not for nothing, what did she think being famous was all about?” and a fourth person added, "I love Chappell Roan but she went too far this time. She’s a celebrity, and paparazzi are just doing their job."

While many disagreed with her actions, a few fans rushed to her defense, with one writing, “She’s just documenting abuse not seeking clout.”

Someone chimed in, “chappell ROAN filming PAPARAZZI in paris is exactly how she should HANDLE the disrespect. she DESERVES her PRIVACY.”

This isn’t the first occasion Chappell has stood her ground against the media.

While attending the 2024 MTV VMAs, she clapped back at a photographer, telling them to “shut the f— up” after hearing the expletive-ridden phrase when she turned away from the camera.

While asked about the incident, she told Entertainment Tonight, “It’s quite overwhelming and quite scary. I think for someone who gets… a lot of… anxiety around people yelling at you, the carpet is horrifying.”
