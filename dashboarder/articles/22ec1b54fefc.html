OnePlus has started notifying distributors and retailers that a majority of its stocks will now be sold online, while Realme has let go of its entire offline sales team, industry executives said.

Smartphone companies are struggling to maintain profit margins amid a sharp increase in the cost of components, especially memory and storage where prices have nearly tripled from a year earlier because of high demand from artificial intelligence data centres. Mass-market brands like OnePlus and Realme that operate on smaller margins than the premium names are affected more because of the limited room to absorb the impact.

Smartphone retailers ET spoke with said they have been notified by their distributors that OnePlus would be shifting most of its inventory to online channels. That would mark a rollback of the previous strategy where the company in 2024 started expanding its offline presence by tapping Reliance Retail's distribution arm, JioMart, to reach retailers.

The South India Organised Retailers Association, which represents franchise-based stores across South India, recently wrote to OnePlus, warning that this move would harm the company's relation with retailers.

ET has seen a copy of the letter. An industry executive aware of OnePlus's move said it was purely a cost-saving measure. "Operating expenses for offline sales are 20-24% of the product cost, whereas online operations only cost 8-10%. Saving this margin will allow the brand to offer highly competitive pricing," he told ET.

On Realme, industry executives said, it laid off 55-60 people in the offline sales team and some of them have been asked to shift to the payrolls of the distributors. Over the last few months, the brand has been reducing its fixed operational costs by outsourcing marketing and in-store promoters to third-party agents. Since beginning of 2026, it has been operating as a sub-brand under Oppo and not as an independent brand. Both Realme and Oppo are owned by China's BBK Electronics. Realme declined to respond to ET's email seeking comment.

The change in strategies comes amid widespread expectations of a sharp decline in handset sales volumes in 2026.
