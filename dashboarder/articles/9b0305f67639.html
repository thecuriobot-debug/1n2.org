Among the six-member Gulf Cooperation Council, the UAE is currently the only economy running a fiscal surplus, recording a budget excess of 4.8% of GDP in 2024. The UAE economy grew 5.1% in the first nine months of 2025, with non-oil activities accounting for three-fourths of GDP.

Economists highlighted the UAE's stronger fiscal health compared to many of its neighbours. "The starting point for the UAE is very strong because the country has been running fiscal surpluses and has substantial sovereign assets," said Ana Nacvalovaite, research fellow at the University of Oxford.

Fiscal buffers offer protection

The UAE's deep SWF pockets could also offer enough cushion. Its state-owned investors (SOIs) manage assets of more than $2.9 trillion, making the country the world's fourth-richest, according to the 2026 annual report by Global SWF. It is led by the Abu Dhabi Investment Authority (ADIA) with assets under management of $1.18 trillion. Saudi Arabia's sovereign investor PIF manages assets worth $1.2 trillion.

Notably, global ratings agency S&P Global recently reaffirmed UAE's AA/A-1+ sovereign credit rating with a stable outlook.
