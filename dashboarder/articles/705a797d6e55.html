Where Is the Release Date for the Set of Sports Cards I Love?

Manufacturers announce details for their products at different intervals. Currently, Topps and Panini go public with their plans one or two months in advance. So if you don’t at least see a release date above, there’s a good chance it’s at least a couple of months away.

Until recently, Topps revealed their release dates and information around six months out. That appears to have changed as Fanatics takes over more of the day-to-day.

Upper Deck’s sports card release dates are usually several months out as well. That said, these often change and, historically, get pushed back.

If a set isn’t listed above, that does not mean that it isn’t happening. This is especially true for established brands that have been around for many years. It’s more likely that the set simply hasn’t been announced yet.

Why Do Sports Card Release Dates Change and Get Delayed?

Manufacturers don’t typically give specific reasons why release dates get pushed back. Not every set is the same, as well. Autographs are one of the culprits. If one of the companies has a set almost ready to go and are expecting some significant or significant number of autographs to be returned shortly, a release date might shift to reduce the number of redemptions. Card companies don’t like them, either.

Supply chain issues are still causing some havoc. Some of the materials needed for modern sports cards have been in short supply. While things seem to be improving, the manufacturers and printers are still clawing their way back to a normal schedule. The hobby’s growth has also meant more demand for production facilities as well with some sets getting larger print runs.

These are just a couple of reasons why the sports card release calendar if constantly shifting.
