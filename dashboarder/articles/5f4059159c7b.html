The decision to conduct the review next week was taken at a meeting chaired by Union home minister Amit Shah on Friday. The meeting was attended by National Security Adviser Ajit Doval, home secretary Govind Mohan, Intelligence Bureau director Tapan Deka and senior Home Ministry officials, sources said.

After the attack, Shah had called Abdullah to inform him that an inquiry had been ordered. Abdullah said on Thursday that the Union minister had expressed concern and assured him he would keep him informed about the progress of the investigation.

A Z-plus protectee is entitled to at least half a dozen NSG commandos, apart from two to three security rings, including local police and other security personnel, besides a bulletproof vehicle and escort.

It has been reported that the National Security Guard will undertake such a review for its protectees.
