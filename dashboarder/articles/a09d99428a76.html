Iran war: Trump calls for securing Hormuz as conflict widens

- Iranian foreign minister threatens attacks on facilities of American companies if its energy facilities are attacked, state media reports

- Abbas Araghchi's comments come after US bombed military targets on Iran's Kharg Island but spared its oil infrastructure

- Separately, Araghchi told MS NOW there was "no problem" with Iran's new supreme leader Mojtaba Khamenei after US officials said he was wounded

Here is a roundup of the main developments in the US-Israeli war with Iran on Saturday, March 14:

Israel to partially resume schools in some areas as war continues

Israel on Monday is expected to allow schools in more rural parts of the country with sufficient bomb shelter facilities to partially resume, as the war continues into the third week. Some parts of southern rural Israel have been less impacted by Iranian missile fire.

However, schools in heavily populated parts of central Israel will remain shut. Educational facilities in northern Israel will also remain closed, as those areas could be hit by Hezbollah in Lebanon.

Trump says securing Strait of Hormuz should be 'team effort'

In a post on Truth Social, Trump again boasted of US military attacks on Iran, saying the US "has beaten and completely decimated Iran, both militarily, economically and in every other way."

"But the countries of the world that receive oil through the Hormuz Strait must take care of that passage, and we will help — A LOT!" Trump added.

"The US will also coordinate with those Countries so that everything goes quickly, smoothly, and well," Trump said, adding that "this should have always been a team efforts, and now it will be."

Trump's comments came hours after he said he hoped countries like China, Japan, France and others would send warships to the waterway.

Israel says 400 airstrike waves hit Iran targets

Israel's military said it has carried out around 400 waves of airstrikes in the first two weeks of the war with Iran.

The Israel Defense Forces said the fighter jets' main targets were missile launch sites and defense installations across central and western Iran.

In just the past 24 hours, more than 200 targets were struck, including dozens of ballistic missile launch pads, some allegedly being prepared for attacks on Israel. Air defense systems and weapons depots were also hit, the military said.

The IDF added that a strike in Tehran on Friday killed two senior intelligence officers from Iran's Khatam al-Anbiya Central Headquarters, which takes command of the country's armed forces in wartime.

UAE condemns attack on consulate in Iraqi Kurdistan

The United Arab Emirates said its consulate in the autonomous Kurdish region of Iraq was targeted by a drone attack.

"The United Arab Emirates has strongly condemned and denounced the unprovoked terrorist drone attack that targeted the UAE Consulate General in the Kurdistan Region of Iraq for the second time in the week," the UAE Foreign Ministry said in a statement.

The UAE said the attack "constitutes a flagrant violation of international norms and laws."

The UAE urged the Iraqi government and Kurdish authorities to probe the circumstances surrounding the attack.

Iran says new supreme leader able to lead

Iran's foreign minister says there is "no problem" with the country’s new supreme leader Mojtaba Khamenei after US officials said he was wounded.

US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth on Friday said he believed Khamenei, who assumed power after his father Ali Khamenei was killed in a strike early in the US-Israel war on Iran, was "wounded and likely disfigured."

Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi told MS NOW (formerly MSNBC) that the new leader remains able to govern. "There is no problem with the new supreme leader. He sent his message yesterday, and he will perform his duties," he said.

Iranian officials had confirmed that Khamenei was wounded but have not provided further details.

The 56-year-old has not appeared in public since the airstrike that killed his father and several family members.

In a written statement Thursday, Khamenei said his wife, sister, her child and his brother-in-law were also killed and vowed retaliation against the US and Israel. He also warned Iran could disrupt regional oil supplies and activate allied forces across the region.

Iranian media says 15 killed in attack on Isfahan factory

Iranian media reported that 15 people were killed in a missile strike on a factory in the city of Isfahan.

The semi-official Fars news agency blamed the US and Israel for the attack. The agency said the facility produces heaters and refrigerators and that employees were inside the building at the time of the strike.

Neither Israel or the US have yet commented on the attack.

Trump says 'many countries' will dispatch warships to keep Hormuz open

US President Donald Trump, in a post on his Truth Social platform, said "many countries" along with the US will send warships to keep the Strait of Hormuz "open and safe."

Trump said he wants China, France, Japan, South Korea and the UK to dispatch ships to the region.

"In the meantime, the United States will be bombing the h--- out of the shoreline, and continually shooting Iranian boats and ships out of the water," the president said.

The Strait of Hormuz, between Iran and Oman, allows oil and natural gas tankers to leave the UAE, Qatar and other energy-rich countries and sail to other regions such as East Asia.

Tankers moving through the Strait in recent days have faced attacks with Iran threatening ships amid the ongoing US-Israeli campaign.

Countries such as China, India, Pakistan South Korea and Japan could face major disruption to their economies if disruption continues.

Israel and Lebanon expected to hold direct talks in coming days, Hareetz reports

Israeli newspaper Haaretz reported that Israel and Lebanon were expected to hold direct talks in the coming days, their first since the outbreak of the Iran war that drew Lebanon deeper into the conflict. The newspaper cited sources familiar with the matter.

The report said Jared Kushner, the son-in-law of US President Donald Trump, would be involved in the talks, which may take place in Paris or Cyprus.

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's close ally Ron Dermer is expected to lead the Israeli delegation.

UN chief urges diplomacy as Israel-Lebanon talks loom

UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres says diplomatic channels remain open to end the war between Israel and the Iran-backed Hezbollah group, urging stronger international support for Lebanon.

Speaking during a visit to Beirut, Guterres said there is "no military solution, only diplomacy, dialogue and full implementation of the UN Charter and Security Council resolutions."

He added that diplomatic avenues remain available through the UN's special coordinator for Lebanon and key member states.

"My message to the international community is simply step up your engagement, empower the Lebanese state and support the Lebanese Armed Forces to secure the capabilities and resources they need," he added.

Lebanon says Israeli strikes have killed 826

Lebanon's Health Ministry says Israeli strikes have killed at least 826 people since March 2.

The ministry said the dead include 65 women and 106 children, while another 2,009 people have been wounded.

A statement added that 31 paramedics were among those killed, after the bodies of additional health workers were recovered following an overnight strike authorities said hit a healthcare centre in southern Lebanon.

Iran threatens to attack facilities of US companies in region — state media

Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi said Tehran would attack the facilities of US companies in the Middle East if Washington targets Iranian energy infrastructure.

Araghchi was cited by state media as saying that Iran would exercise caution and avoid targeting populated areas.

Earlier on Saturday, the United States said it had hit military targets on Kharg Island, Iran's main oil export hub.

Iran's semi-official Fars news agency said that the strikes had caused no damage to oil infrastructure.

US President Donald Trump has threatened to hit Iranian oil infrastructure unless Tehran stops blocking the Strait of Hormuz, a major passage for oil deliveries.

The war between the US and Iran has sent global oil prices skyrocketing.

Trump says tanker planes largely undamaged after strike

US President Donald Trump says a group of refueling tanker planes hit in an attack on a base in Saudi Arabia suffered "virtually no damage."

In a post on Truth Social, Trump said four aircraft were already back in service and that a fifth had only slightly more damage and would return to the air shortly.

Trump criticized US media reports, including a Wall Street Journal article that said Iranian strikes had damaged five of the planes on the ground at Prince Sultan Air Base.

The Journal reported that no casualties occurred and that the aircraft were undergoing repairs.
