Here are the top headlines from Germany on Saturday, March 14, 2026:

Wildberger calls for looser EU regulations on AI

German Digital Minister Karsten Wildberger said that an EU plan to loosen regulations on artificial intelligence didn't go far enough.

"I strongly warn against losing sight of the actual goal in the reforms to the AI Act: We need more relief and more freedom for AI developers," Wildberger told the Reuters news agency.

"We were able to achieve substantial improvements in the negotiations, but the proposal needs significantly more courage," he said.

Wildberger said that Europe could miss a "great opportunity" to increase competitiveness by making use of "cutting-edge technologies," adding that Berlin will continue to advocate the removal of unnecessary hurdles to innovation.

Companies in the EU have criticized the bloc, saying that its strict regulations have stifled innovation in the tech sector as Europe falls behind the US and China in the field of AI.

US easing of Russia oil sanctions 'geostrategic disaster' — Greens' Brantner

Franziska Brantner, one of the two co-leaders of the opposition environmentalist Greens, called for Germany and other European Union member states to take stronger measures against Russia's shadow fleet of oil tankers.

"Germany and Europe must now use their own levers against Putin's war chest and stop the passage of these shadow tankers through our waters," Brantner told Germany's DPA news agency.

Russia has operated a clandestine network of tankers without clear documentation of ownership to evade international sanctions imposed due to its invasion of Ukraine.

Brantner also criticized the United States' easing of sanctions on Russian oil, describing it as a "geostrategic disaster" that would allow Moscow to increase its revenues.

She said that Ukraine was fighting for freedom in Europe and must continue to receive support.

The United States temporarily eased oil sanctions on Russia on Friday in an effort to bring energy prices down amid Washington's war with Iran and Tehran's blockage of the Strait of Hormuz, which is a major passage for oil deliveries.

German Chancellor Friedrich Merz also criticized the move on Friday, saying, "Now is not the time to ease sanctions."

In January, Belgium seized a vessel it suspected of belonging to Russia's shadow fleet.

15-year-old steals public bus in central Germany

A 15-year-old boy stole a public bus in the town of Wiesbaden in the central state of Hesse and drove it across state lines to Karlsruhe in southwestern Baden-Württemberg, police said on Saturday.

The joyride covered some 150 kilometers (93 miles).

According to police, the boy's 14-year-old girlfriend was also on board the bus when a police patrol stopped the vehicle in Karlsruhe.

The boy took control of the fully fueled bus using a master key on Friday morning while it was parked at a depot in Wiesbaden.

It was initially unclear how the master key ended up in the teenager's possession. The vehicle was returned undamaged to the bus operator and the 15-year-old was handed over to his guardians.

While bus operator employees noticed the bus was missing early in the morning, the theft was only reported to police around noun as they had initially assumed that a driver had simply taken the wrong vehicle.

Criminal proceedings have been initiated against the teenager for theft and driving without a license.

Government to halt funding of independent asylum counseling providers — reports

German Interior Minister Alexander Dobrindt of the ruling conservative CDU/CSU plans to cut funds for independent counseling services for asylum seekers, the RND media group and the investigative journalism group Correctiv reported.

Independent counseling for asylum procedures inform asylum seekers about their rights in Germany.

According to the reports, counseling providers were informed of the planned cuts to the 2027 budget on Monday.

The Workers' Welfare Association (AWO) charity described the move as a "serious political mistake."

"After all the attacks on the right to asylum, civil society is now actively being forced out of supporting refugees," AWO chief Michael Groß told RND.

The Pro Asyl rights group also criticized the planned cuts.

"Independent asylum procedure advice is not a luxury, but a central safeguard under the rule of law in asylum proceedings," Pro Asyl managing director Karl Kopp told RND.

Dobrindt has called for Germany to tighten border controls and crack down on "abuse" of asylum law.

SPD says cuts not agreed upon by coalition

Hakan Demir, a lawmaker for the CDU/CSU's junior coalition partner, the center-left Social Democrats (SPD), told Correctiv that the move had not yet been agreed upon by the coalition.

Demir said that it was important for "vulnerable groups" to have access to information from sources other than government officials.

"We therefore continue to advocate for independent asylum procedure counseling," he said.

The plan was also criticized by the opposition environmentalist Greens.

"Well-informed refugees lead to better asylum decisions — regardless of whether an application is accepted or rejected," Greens lawmaker Filiz Polat told Correctiv.

She added that independent counseling leads to fairer and speedier proceedings with fewer appeals.

What is independent asylum counseling?

Asylum seekers in Germany have access to free counseling funded by the state.

State funding for the program was approved by former Chancellor Olaf Scholz's government in 2022 in a bid to accelerate proceedings for asylum requests.

Scholz belongs to the SPD and governed in coalition with the Greens and the pro-business Free Democrats (FDP).

Counseling has been provided through civil society organizations since mid-2023.

Some 108,000 asylum seekers made use of the program in the first two years of its existence, according to RND.

Weekend cold snap hits Germany

Early spring weather has given way to a cold snap in Germany, with chilly, humid air flowing in from the west.

According to the German weather service (DWD), some 400-600 meters (around 15,700 - 23,600 inches) of snow fell in the western parts of the Mittelgebirge highlands and in the Harz Mountains overnight.

Snow was forecast on Saturday in those regions as well as in the southwestern Black Forest area.

However, warmer temperatures were expected to return by early next week.

"We expect another band of rain to move across Germany on Monday night. Starting on Tuesday, however, it will get significantly milder and drier," DWD meteorologist Thorsten Kaluza told the Bild daily.
