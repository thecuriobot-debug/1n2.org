Doctor performs groundbreaking robotic surgery from 1,500 miles away

A surgeon in London has successfully performed the UK's first long-distance robotic surgery on a patient in Gibraltar. Experts say the breakthrough could improve access to specialised healthcare for patients in remote areas.

In a remarkable medical milestone, a surgeon in London has successfully performed a robotic operation on a patient located about 1,500 miles away in Gibraltar.

The procedure marks the United Kingdom’s first long-distance robotic surgery and is being hailed as a major step forward in the future of healthcare.

Experts believe such technology could make specialised surgical care more accessible to patients living in remote or underserved regions.

The surgery was carried out by Professor Prokar Dasgupta, a leading urological surgeon based in London.

Using advanced robotic technology and a high-speed digital connection, he was able to control surgical instruments in real time while the patient remained in a hospital in Gibraltar.

According to Professor Dasgupta, the successful procedure demonstrates how modern technology can bridge geographical barriers in medicine. “This is good news for patients in remote areas,” he said, noting that highly skilled surgeons could treat patients without needing to travel long distances.

Robotic surgery itself is not new, but performing it over such a long distance presents unique challenges.

The system requires extremely stable internet connectivity and precise control to ensure there is no delay between the surgeon’s movements and the robot’s response.

Medical experts say the breakthrough could transform global healthcare in the future. Remote robotic surgeries could allow top specialists to treat patients anywhere in the world, reduce waiting times, and improve access to advanced medical procedures.

While more testing and regulations will be needed before the technology becomes widely available, the successful operation has opened the door to a new era of remote healthcare.
